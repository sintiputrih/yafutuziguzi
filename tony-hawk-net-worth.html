Tony Hawk | Charity

Hawk has always been generous while giving back to the people and society. Being a skater, he motivates people and provides the platform to skate.

In 2002, he established Tony Hawk Foundation and initiated the campaign to combat America’s lack of safe and legal skateparks.

In 2015, Tony’s foundation received the Robert Wood Johnson Sports award for its immense contribution to improving the health and safety of underserved communities.

Meanwhile, his foundation has donated more than $5.8 million to improve the safety of more than 576 skateparks across the country.

In addition, Tony is co-founder of Athletes for Hope, which helps inspire athletes to volunteer in their communities.

Tony Hawk | Career

Hawk started his pro career at 14 with the Power Peralta team. By the time, he had endorsements from Theeve and Quicksilver.

By 16, he got a name for himself as one of the best skaters in the world.

Meanwhile, Tony made several records in skating and ruled the skating world for more than 12 years, with more than 70 competition wins over the course of his successful career.

He invented many moves during his glittering career, including the gymnast plant, ollie-to-Indy, and saran wrap.

Likewise, after introducing the X Games in 1995, Tony won ten golds, three silver, and two bronze in the X Games.

And, he was the first skater to land 900, which means skateboarder rotates 900 degrees while being in the air.

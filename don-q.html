Don Q – Professional Life and Career

He started his career by participating in several rap battles around his community. Some of the rap battles which he completed are “Battle on the Beat” and “Come Up Groundz”.

He signed to Highbridge the Label with A Boogie with da Boogie and Trap Many in 2016. Highbridge the Label made a partnership with Atlantic Records. After one year of partnership, Don Q released his debut mixtape named “Don Season”. This mixtape was hosted by DJ Don Cannon and has features from A Boogie, Keir, and PnB Rock. In 2016, he collaborated with several artists like Meek Mill, Young Scooter, Fabolous, Dave East, and many more.

He released his second mixtape “Corber Stories” in 2017 featuring A Boogie, PnB Rock, Fabolous, Zoey Dollaz, Jadakiss, Styles P, and Loso Loaded. In addition, Don Q is also featured in A Boogie’s first album “The Bigger Artist”. In the year, he dropped his next mixtape titled “Don Talk”.

In 2018, He released his extended version of the mixtape “Don Season” named “Don Season 2”. Similarly, he battled with Joyner Lucas in 2019 in response to Tory Lanez’s challenge and claimed himself the best rapper. Don dropped his diss track named “I’m not Joyner” to which Lanez responded with the next diss track “Don Queen”. The next day,  He replied with his diss track “This is Ya King?” However, He got banned by the NYPD.

On July 1, 2020, He released “Flood My Wrist” and on September 2, 2020, he released “Vroom Vroom” in collaboration with A Boogie.

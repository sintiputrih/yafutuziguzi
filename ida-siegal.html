Ida Siegal is a journalist and news reporter from the United States of America. Moreover, Ida Siegal has worked as an on-air news reporter at NBC New York since January 2003.

Ida Siegal studied and completed her schooling at a local school in her hometown. After that, she enrolled at the Newhouse School of Public Communication at Syracuse University where she graduated with magna cum laude a double major in broadcast journalism and public policy.

Professional life

Ida Siegal started her career as an intern at NBC Asia and lived in Hong Kong in the year 1999. After that, she continued to work as an intern at NBC Asia until she joined WNBC. Moreover, she then worked as a reporter and anchor at News 12 in the Bronx. She covered several stories like the trial of police officers indicted for the killing of Amadou Diallo and the attacks that occurred on September 11 during her time at News 12.

Moreover, she also served as a reporter for News 12 Long Island where she covered education, politics, and crime while at News 12 in the Bronx. She joined NBC New York in 2003 and has spent most of her career reporting in the Tri-State area. Moreover, she covered heartbreaking fires, hurricanes, blizzards, and accidents while working as a reporter for NBC New York.

Additionally, she kept her smiles through countless feature stories shining a light on the best of the human spirit. Besides that, she also covered the shooting at Sandy Hook Elementary School, the 2016 Presidential election, and the fatal Metro-North Crash in Riverdale. She published her first chapter of the children’s book series in 2015 with Scholastic titled “Emma Is In The Air”.

Moreover, her book tells the story of an intrepid reporter who at 8 started solving mysteries at school and broadcasted her news reports to all her friends. She started writing children’s books after the curious children she met in the community who wanted to know what it’s like to be a reporter. Currently, she works as a general assignment reporter for NBC 4 New York starting at 4 p.m. on weekday newscasts and starting at 6 p.m. on Sunday evenings.

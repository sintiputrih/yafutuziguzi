Karim El Hakim is a multi-award-winning producer and cinematographer who is half Egyptian and half Swedish-American.

Karim's work in the Middle East has centered on exposing social issues, development challenges, and marginalized societies.

He witnessed the Egyptian Revolution firsthand and was able to capture it minute by minute as it happened in his downtown Cairo neighborhood.

His film about the Egyptian revolution, "1/2 REVOLUTION," was nominated for Best World Cinema Documentary at the 2012 Sundance Film Festival and won Best Film at the Aljazeera Documentary Film Festival in 2012.

Karim has also worked as a cameraman/DOP on international political documentaries, including Philippe Dib's In Search of Oil and Sand and Alex Gibney's My Trip To Alqaeda.

He is a self-taught cinematographer with over 25 years of freelance experience who studied architecture and the philosophy of art and architecture at Tufts University.

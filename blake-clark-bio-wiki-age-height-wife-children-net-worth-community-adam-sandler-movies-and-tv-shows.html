Blake Clark Biography

Blake Clark is an American Actor, Stand-Up Comedian, and Voice Actor who was born and brought up in Macon, Georgia, United States of America. He is widely known for his roles in the sports comedy film ‘The Waterboy’ as Farmer Fran, the ABC television sitcom ‘Home Improvement’ as Harry “The Hardware Store Guy”, and in the fantasy comedy film ‘Little Nicky’ as Jimmy the Demon.

Blake is also known for his voicing the character Slinky Dog, in the Disney franchise ‘Toy Story’ which include ‘Toy Story 3’, ‘Toy Story 4’, ‘Toy Story 3: The Video Game’ and the ‘Toy Story: The Musical’.

Blake has appeared on other notable films and series which include ‘Boy Meets World’ as Chet Hunter, ‘Lost at Home’ as Ralphie / Hotdog Vendor and in ‘Between Two Ferns: The Movie’ as Earl Canderton. As a Stand-Up Comedian, he has appeared on the club circuit, “The Tonight Show”, the HBO comedy specials, and on Late Night with Conan O’Brien.

    Blake Clark Career

    Clark is a veteran of the Vietnam War, having filled in as a first lieutenant in the United States Army with the fifth Infantry Division. He is frequently cast in numerous Adam Sandler films including The Waterboy, Mr. Deeds, 50 First Dates, Eight Crazy Nights, Little Nicky, I Now Pronounce You Chuck and Larry, Grown Ups, Bedtime Stories, and That’s My Boy.

    Additionally, he has appeared in numerous television guest appearances including  Boy Meets World, Home Improvement, The Drew Carey Show, The Jamie Foxx Show, and Girl Meets World.

    Also in Remington Steele, he was Fred the chauffeur. In place of Jim Varney, Clark is the current voice of Slinky Dog in the Toy Story franchise. Jim was Slinky’s original voice actor in the first two movies, who died in 2000 of lung cancer. In real life, he and Varney were good friends.

    Blake Clark Army

    Shortly after graduating from college Blake Clark’s career was sidetracked when he was called on to serve his country in the Vietnam War. He was assigned to the 5th infantry division where he worked his way up to becoming a first Lieutenant. During his service time, he was a decorated platoon leader. When he started his stand-up career he would joke about his time in the war. In one part of his routine, he would talk about having flashbacks of Georgia while in Vietnam.

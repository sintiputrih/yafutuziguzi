Chicago Fire has successfully made it to 10 seasons, and its casts — who are always diving headfirst into danger to save innocent lives — each has made a separate place in the viewers’ hearts. Among them, Heather Darden, portrayed by the actress Chaon Cross, was one of the most interesting characters because of her shocking life choices.

But who is she in real life? What are is age, and measurement? Is she married? Find the answers as this Chaon Cross Wikipedia proceeds.

Chaon Cross On Chicago Fire

Chaon portrayed the role of Heather Darden, the widow of Andy Darden. She was an important character on Chicago Fire who blamed the death of her husband on Kelly Severide. Though unjustified, Heather felt that if Kelly hadn’t convinced her husband to pursue a career as a firefighter, he would still be alive and well today.

Then one day, to distract herself from the pain, Heather goes out to drink with her girlfriends. But little did she know her life was going to change that day. She crashes her car and one of her best friends dies from the impact. 

Also, because she drove under the influence, she get charged with a DUI, vehicular manslaughter, and ultimately receives a 15-month prison sentence.

After serving her time, Heather then comes out of the prison, only to break another law and go back straight to prison.

Trivia: Chaon’s on-screen husband, Andrew Darden was portrayed by Corey Sorenson. The pair shared two on-screen sons named Griffin and Ben Darden.

Chaon Cross Career

Chaon Cross is an actress who has appeared in TV shows like Next (2020), The Exorcist (2016), Boss (2012), Detroit 1-8-7 (2010), and not to mention Chicago Fire (since 2013). Also, she got a small screen time in the movie Windows (2018) as a sauna woman.

Apart from this, Chaon has worked for various theatre companies including Lookingglass Theatre Company (Chicago, Illinois),  Milwaukee Repertory Theater (Milwaukee, Wisconsin), Northlight Theatre (Skokie, Illinois), Court Theatre (Chicago, Illinois),  and Writers Theatre (Glencoe, Illinois).

As for her education, Chaon went on to receive her BFA in Theatre Arts from Stephens College in 1997. Then she did a semester at Oxford University, Oxford, England, and also received private tuition from John Kane at RSC, Stratford Upon Avon, England.

Furthermore, she has trained in ballet for 3 yrs with Michael Simms, and Modern/Expressive/Jazz dance for 5 years with Pamela Jones.

She has soprano/alto vocals, and can play guitar, juggle, ride a horse, and tumble.

Mentioning her achievements, Chaon received the 2019 Equity Joseph Jefferson Award for “Performer in a Principal Role” for the play “Photography 51” at the Court Theatre in Chicago, Illinois. Also, she was previously nominated for Equity Joseph Jefferson Award for the play “Proof” in 2013.

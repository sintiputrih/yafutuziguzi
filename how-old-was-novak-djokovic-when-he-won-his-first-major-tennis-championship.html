How Old Was Novak Djokovic When he Won His First Major Tennis Championship?

By Keith Mado July 12, 2024 11:57

Photo by Matthew Stockman / Getty Images

Novak Djokovic, a Serbian professional tennis player and world No. 1 in men's singles for over two years, started playing tennis when he was only four on May 22nd, 1987, at his birthplace, Belgrade, Serbia. This was early evidence of his talent and commitment to basketball as he moved up from the grassroots levels with impressive skills that showed how determined he was on the basketball court.

Djokovic quickly ascended to the upper levels of competitive tennis, reaching the French Open semifinals and Wimbledon quarterfinals in 2007 before making his first Grand Slam final at that year's US Open, where Roger Federer defeated him. It was a watershed moment in his career, suggesting he could compete at the highest level of professional tennis.

The First Grand Slam Triumph

In his career, Djokovic won 17 Grand Slam titles, the first one being at the Australian Open in 2008. Djokovic beat Jo-Wilfried Tsonga in the final (22nd) and showed remarkable maturity and ability on the court at 20 years of age. This win reinforced that he was one of the future big things in tennis and paved the way for his domination over men's professional tennis.

Why Djokovic Winning His First Major at 20 Means a Lot

When he won the 2008 Australian Open at just 20 years of age, Djokovic became one of the youngest Grand Slam champions in tennis history. Lady Luck treated him well in his early career at the starting gate, announcing itself as a promising facet of the high-level company he would keep for many years. It signified the arrival of a new tennis era, with Djokovic at its forefront.

How he Stacks up With Other Elite Tennis Players

After winning his first grand slam title at age 20, Novak Djokovic has been compared to some of the game's top tennis players. In many respects, the early triumphs of players like Rafael Nadal and Roger Federer resemble those moments experienced by Djokovic. Those comparisons only demonstrate men's professional tennis's exceptional quality and depth.

On the Whole

When Novak Djokovic captured his debut major crown at the 2008 Australian Open as a fresh-faced 20-year-old, it represented a turning point in his career. It showed his incredible talent, competitiveness, and a guy who could be one of the greatest players ever in history. This milestone carries so much weight through the rest of Djokovic's career, affirming his place as a tennis force and opening up space for him to stamp an even bigger legacy down.

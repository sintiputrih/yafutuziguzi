Loni Love from 'The Real' Shares Photo of Herself Dressed up in Black &amp; Reveals She's a UNCF Ambassa

June 2024 · 3 minute read

American comic, Loni Love, shared a series of new photos on her Instagram page, announcing that she became an ambassador of the American philanthropic organization, UNCF.

In an exciting turn of event, Loni Love took to her Instagram page yesterday to share the news of her ambassadorship to the United Negro College Fund.

Loni Love poses for portrait at 45th Daytime Emmy Awards - Portraits by The Artists Project Sponsored by the Visual Snow Initiative | Photo: Getty Images

The female comedian gave fans a peek of the pearl-embellished black top that she wore as she posed against a UNCF board in the first snapshot while the second showed her and actor Anthony Anderson posing for the camera.

UNCF is the nation's largest and most effective minority education organization that plays a vital role in subsidizing tuition for students and providing them with scholarship opportunities, per Shaw University's website.

According to Information Cradle, the Emmy Award-winning comedian was born on July 12, 1971, in Detroit, Michigan, U.S.

Aside from her budding comedy career, Loni went on to become a host on "The Real" talk show along with Jeannie Mai, Tamera Mowry, and Adrienne Bailon.

When asked who her favorite guest on the show was during an interview with Refinery 29, she said it was J.Lo, explaining that she [J.Lo] brought quite a bit of spunk and class to the show.

It hasn't been all rosy with Loni and the women of "The Real" talk show, as the television host was accused of plotting to get Tamar Braxton fired from "The Real" in 2016.
